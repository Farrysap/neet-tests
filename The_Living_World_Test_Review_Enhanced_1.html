The Living World - NEET Review Test (20 Questions)

Time Left: 20:00

Q1. Which of the following is NOT considered a characteristic of living organisms?

Explanation: Crystallization is a physical process and occurs in non-living matter. It does not define life.

Q2. The term 'taxonomy' was coined by:

Explanation: AP de Candolle coined the term 'taxonomy' which is the science of classification.

Q3. Binomial nomenclature includes:

Explanation: In binomial nomenclature, the scientific name is composed of the Genus name followed by the species name.

Q4. Which one of the following is the correct sequence in biological classification hierarchy?

Explanation: The correct hierarchy from smallest to broader is: Species < Genus < Family < Order.

Q5. The repository of dried plant specimens for study and reference is called:

Explanation: A herbarium is a collection of preserved plant specimens used for research and reference.

Q6. Which taxonomic aid is primarily used for the identification of plants and animals based on contrasting characters?

Explanation: A 'key' is used for identifying organisms using a series of choices based on contrasting characters.

Q7. Which of the following names is correctly written according to binomial nomenclature?

Explanation: The genus name starts with a capital letter and the species name is in lowercase. Both are italicized or underlined.

Q8. Who is regarded as the father of taxonomy?

Explanation: Carolus Linnaeus is known as the father of taxonomy for his work on classification and naming of organisms.

Q9. Which one of the following is a category in the taxonomic hierarchy?

Explanation: 'Genus' is a taxonomic category used in classification.

Q10. ICBN deals with the nomenclature of:

Explanation: The International Code of Botanical Nomenclature (ICBN) governs naming of plants.

Q11. Which of the following taxonomic categories ranks higher than 'family'?

Explanation: 'Order' is a higher taxonomic rank than 'family'. The sequence is: Species < Genus < Family < Order.

Q12. Growth in living organisms is accompanied by:

Explanation: Growth in living organisms usually involves both an increase in mass and an increase in number of individuals.

Q13. The term systematics was introduced by:

Explanation: Linnaeus used the term Systema Naturae to describe his classification system, thus popularizing the term 'systematics'.

Q14. Which of the following exhibits both growth and reproduction but is not considered truly living?

Explanation: Viruses can reproduce inside a host and show crystalline growth, but they are considered non-living outside a host.

Q15. Which one of the following organisms is correctly matched with its taxonomic aid?

Explanation: A herbarium stores dried plant specimens, typically herbs, for reference and classification.

Q16. Which of the following is NOT a correct feature of binomial nomenclature?

Explanation: Only the Genus name starts with a capital letter; species name starts with a lowercase letter.

Q17. Which domain of life includes prokaryotic organisms that can live in extreme environments?

Explanation: Archaea are prokaryotes that can survive in extreme conditions like hot springs, salty lakes, etc.

Q18. Which of the following is a taxonomic category immediately higher than species?

Explanation: Genus comes immediately above species in taxonomic hierarchy.

Q19. Which component of taxonomy includes the process of naming organisms?

Explanation: Nomenclature is the branch that deals with assigning names to organisms following specific rules.

Q20. Taxonomic keys are based on:

Explanation: Keys are based on contrasting characters which help in identifying organisms correctly.
